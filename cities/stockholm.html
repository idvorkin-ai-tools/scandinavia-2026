Stockholm

Sweden · the longest stop · with the Saffitzes
MapStockholm on Google Maps ↗

Four nights across fourteen islands — the train rolls in around 20:42 on Jul 3, so let day one be a quiet arrival and let the city unspool from there with the Saffitzes.

Start with the postcard set and let getting-around do double duty as sightseeing. Wander Gamla Stan, the old town, then peek inside the Royal Palace; tour City Hall (the blue Nobel-banquet one). The Vasa warship museum is the don't-miss — and the fun way in is to sail across the harbor to Djurgården rather than walk. While you're on the island, the ABBA museum is right there, and an archipelago cruise out among the islands is worth a half-day if the weather holds. The vintage tram on the Djurgårdslinjen is its own little tour: it runs Fri & Sat between Norrmalmstorg and Djurgården (check djurgardslinjen.se for the schedule; SL ticket or tap, 43 SEK each), threading past Strandvägen for food and drinks, Djurgårdsbron for boat-watching, the Vasa museum, Skansen (Nordic animals + historic Sweden), and Gröna Lund amusement park. Underground, run a metro-station art tour — one line at a time — and stack the viewpoints: the skyline from the top of Katarinahissen and the city-and-sea panorama walking Fjällgatan, plus Monteliusvägen and Skinnarviksberget.

The other half of the trip is eating and wandering. Graze the food halls — grand Östermalms Saluhall and lively Hötorgshallen (where the noodles run cheap) — and hit the casual spots: Günther's sausages, Dubbla deli sandwiches, Ni Hao bento & dumplings, Japanese sandwiches, Ai Ramen Sofia, Mormor's dumplings, and a bowl of ox beef noodles. Stock a picnic at Cajsa Warg, Eataly, or a Coop, then sift the second-hand racks at Agape Second Hand and Qud Varnet. Greenery is everywhere: walk to the royal pavilions in Haga Park, take the 2 km wooden Baltic promenade in Nacka Strand, catch sunset at Ivar Lo's Park, and picnic at Bergianska Trädgården. Free and cheap wins round it out — Liljevalchs art museum is free on Mondays, there's a free boat tour in Hammarby Sjöstad, and a short train ride gets you to Konditori Lyran for fika.
