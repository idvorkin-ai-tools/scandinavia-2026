Oslo

Norway · sculptures, museums & the opera roof
MapOslo on Google Maps ↗

Norway opens with a compact capital where world-class museums, a fjord at the doorstep, and a roof you can climb are all within an easy day's walk.

Lead with the icons. Frogner Park is free and open at all hours — wander the Vigeland sculptures, the largest sculpture park made by a single artist. Walk up the slanted marble roof of the Oslo Opera House and look out to the sea for fjord views, the city's signature move. The Munch Museum (the new tower in Bjørvika) and the National Museum cover the art; Oslo City Hall is where the Nobel Peace Prize is awarded each December and is worth stepping inside for the famous murals. Save a half day for Bygdøy, the museum peninsula reachable by ferry: the Norwegian Folk Museum (Norsk Folkemuseum) with its open-air historic buildings, the Viking Ship Museum, the Fram Museum (polar exploration), and the Kon-Tiki Museum all cluster together — plan on roughly half a day to do it justice.

Then the rest. Akershus Fortress is a 700-year-old medieval castle with harbor views and free grounds. Holmenkollen, the ski jump turned viewpoint, gives you the city from above — and you can take the zipline back down to the bottom if you're game. Deichman Bjørvika is the stunning waterfront public library, right by the Opera House. Eat your way through the food halls: Mathallen Oslo and the Barcode District spots. Grønland is the multicultural quarter — hit Grønland Torg for the fruit & vegetable market. Roseslottet (a hillside art-and-history installation) and the Paradox museum (optical illusions) round out the offbeat picks, and Pan Japanese Cafe up on Sandakerveien is the neighborhood detour for a quiet bite.
