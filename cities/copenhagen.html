Copenhagen

Denmark · palaces, food halls & harbor
MapCopenhagen on Google Maps ↗

Leg two: in from Reykjavík, three nights with Ammon as your home base for a walkable, water-laced city that rewards an early start and a slow wander.

Copenhagen stacks its crowns close together. Lead with the castles and palaces: Amalienborg for the changing of the guard (honestly not that interesting — drop in, don't plan a day around it), Rosenborg with the King's Garden out front, and Kronborg out at Helsingør if you want a day trip with a "Hamlet" backdrop. The move at Christiansborg is the Tower — a 360° view of the whole city at no cost, so go early before the line builds, then poke around the Royal Library gardens right next door. Tivoli is genuinely worth it for an evening. And the free or cheap wins carry the rest of the trip: the Botanical Garden is free except the butterfly and palm houses, Assistens Cemetery is free to just wander, the Round Tower gives you another rooftop angle, and the Glyptoteket pairs indoor gardens with sculpture. Round it out with art at SMK (the National Gallery).

For eating and roaming, build around the food halls and the water. Lunch at Torvehallerne is the reliable hit; Reffen serves street food right by the harbor, and Broens is the spot near Nyhavn — because Nyhavn itself is great to look at but pricey to eat at, so visit, photograph, then walk to Broens. Christiania, the free, self-governing neighborhood, is worth an afternoon, and in Christianshavn cross the Cirkelbroen, a cool pedestrian bridge. Cool off with a swim in the harbor (the water's clean enough to do it). If you want a canal tour, prebook it — they fill. Slot in coffee at the shop on Kampmannsgade 1 between stops.
