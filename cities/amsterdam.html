Amsterdam

Netherlands · the final leg · canals & museums
MapAmsterdam on Google Maps ↗

After the fjords, Amsterdam is the soft landing before home — three flat, walkable nights of canals, bikes, and museums to wind the trip down.

The museums anchor the days. Do the Van Gogh Museum once — it's a one-time pilgrimage, not a re-run — and book the Anne Frank House early, because timed tickets sell out weeks ahead. The Moco is small and spendy and may need a pass, so treat it as optional; if you want more, the Royal Palace on the Dam, the Canal House museum, the Dutch Resistance Museum (Verzetsmuseum), and Micropia — the world's microbe museum — each reward a couple of hours. Between sittings, just walk: lace through the canal ring, lose an afternoon in the Jordaan, drift south into De Pijp, and circle back through leafy Oud West. For a view, head up the A'DAM Lookout and ride the swing out over the edge, and on the last evening cap it with a sunset canal cruise so the city slides past while the light goes gold.

The quirky, local stuff is where Amsterdam gets fun. The Cat Boat (De Poezenboot) is open Fri or Sat 1–3 pm only, six people at a time, moored at Singel 38G — squeeze it in if a window lines up. Take the free ferry from behind Centraal to Amsterdam Noord just to see the other side of the river, hunt down the tiny tea store in the narrowest house in Europe at Oude Hoogstraat 22, and listen for the Kalverstraat clock that goes off every hour on the corner by the Lush store. If you shop, carry your passport: VAT-free claims net you roughly 21% back. And when you want sea air or a change of scene, the trains make day trips easy — Zaandam for the green Zaanse houses, or the North Sea beach at Zandvoort, both a short ride out and back.

The pick-list

  • The Cat Boat / De Poezenboot — open Fri or Sat 1–3 pm only, up to 6 people at a time. Singel 38G, 1015 AB Amsterdam · tel 020 625 87 94 · visiting info ↗
  • Amsterdam Noord — worth the free ferry ride to explore the other side of the city.
  • Wander the neighborhoods — the canal ring, the Jordaan, De Pijp, Oud West.
  • Kalverstraat clock — goes off every hour, on the corner at the Lush store.
  • Tea store in the narrowest house in Europe — Oude Hoogstraat 22.
  • Metro station Rokin — handy central stop on the line.
  • Sea access — take a train from Centraal Station to Zandvoort (North Sea beach).
  • Tax-free VAT refund — shop with your passport and you qualify for ~21% savings.
  • Moco museum — small and spendy, and might need a pass.
  • Zaandam — day trip out for the Zaanse houses.
  • Anne Frank House — book timed tickets well ahead.
  • Sunset canal cruise — golden-hour glide through the canals.
  • Vondelpark and/or the Jordaan — green space and the prettiest streets.
  • Van Gogh Museum — one time only.
  • A'DAM Lookout and swing — top-deck view and the over-the-edge swing.
  • More museums — Royal Palace (city palace), Canal House museum, Dutch Resistance Museum (Verzetsmuseum), Micropia (microbe museum).
