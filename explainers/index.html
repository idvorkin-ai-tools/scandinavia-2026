Cities & Places · 7

The stop pages — the two-paragraph read plus the "why it looks this way" behind each city on the route.

🇮🇸ReykjavíkA young, improvised North Atlantic capital where Viking settlement, Danish rule, a WWII base, Cold War strategy, and live volcanoes all overlap. 🇩🇰CopenhagenA royal harbor capital on the Øresund, burned and bombarded repeatedly, then rebuilt into a welfare-state city of design, bikes, and food. 🇸🇪StockholmBuilt on the choke point where Lake Mälaren meets the Baltic — a medieval trade lock turned imperial power base and design-minded capital. 🇳🇴OsloA compact fjord-and-forest capital where monarchy, maritime trade, oil wealth, and social democracy all sit inside a walkable core. ⛰️VossA working mountain-and-rail town between Bergen and the fjords that turned its hard terrain into Norway's adventure-sports base. 🌉BergenWestern Norway's old trading capital — a Hanseatic stockfish port wedged between the sea and seven mountains, now the gateway to the fjords. 🇳🇱AmsterdamEngineered land — a water-managed port that turned Golden-Age trade into canals, art, and empire, and now manages its own popularity.

How the Nordics Work · 7

Economy and society — the machine behind the calm streets, the high prices, and the world-topping happiness scores.

🤝The Nordic ModelMarket economy plus heavy redistribution, resting on high social trust — the bargain behind why they keep topping the happiness rankings. 🛢️Nordic Oil MoneyNorway made the state the landlord so the public captured the money, then saved it abroad in a $2T fund instead of spending it. 💸Why Everything Costs So MuchWhy the beer is $14 and nobody eats out — high, equal wages are a feature, not a bug, and they price restaurants into a rare treat. 🔓Why Nordic Social Trust Is So HighThe single number that quietly powers the whole model — how many people believe a stranger won't cheat them. ⛓️The Nordic Prison ParadoxNo bars on some windows, unarmed guards who eat with inmates, a 21-year cap — and far fewer people coming back. 🇪🇺Why Norway Said No to the EU — TwiceNorway said no twice yet still uses the single market and Schengen — the contradiction that runs the whole story. ♨️How Iceland Runs on RenewablesGeothermal steam and glacial hydro that heat the houses and smelt the aluminium — the mirror image of Norway's oil.
